### **1. CURRENCY BASICS & EVOLUTION**

#### **Why Currency Inequality Exists**
- **Economic Strength**: Stronger economies = stronger currencies
- **Supply & Demand**: Currency trading determines exchange rates
- **Interest Rates**: Higher rates attract foreign investment
- **Inflation**: Lower inflation preserves currency value
- **Political Stability**: Stable governments support currency strength

#### **Historical Evolution**
**Barter System ‚Üí Gold Standard ‚Üí Fiat Currency ‚Üí Digital Currency**
- **Barter Era**: Goods exchanged for goods
- **Gold Standard**: Currencies backed by gold reserves
- **Bretton Woods**: USD became global reserve currency (1944-1971)
- **Fiat System**: Currencies backed by government trust (post-1971)
- **Modern Era**: Free-floating exchange rates

**üí° Quiz Tip**: USD remains the dominant global reserve currency (~60% of global reserves)!

### **2. MAJOR CURRENCY PAIRS**

#### **USD-INR (US Dollar - Indian Rupee)**
**Key Factors Affecting USD-INR:**
- **Interest Rate Differential**: RBI vs Federal Reserve rates
- **Current Account Deficit**: India's trade balance
- **Foreign Investment**: FII/FDI flows into India
- **Oil Prices**: India imports ~85% of oil needs
- **Economic Growth**: GDP growth differential

**Trading Insights:**
- **Oil Price ‚Üë**: INR weakens (higher import bill)
- **Fed Rate ‚Üë**: USD strengthens against INR
- **FII Inflows ‚Üë**: INR strengthens
- **Current Account Deficit ‚Üë**: INR pressure

#### **EUR-USD (Euro - US Dollar)**
**Key Drivers:**
- **European Central Bank (ECB)** vs **Federal Reserve** policies
- **Economic Data**: GDP, employment, inflation
- **Political Events**: Brexit, EU elections
- **Risk Sentiment**: Safe haven demand for USD

#### **GBP-USD (British Pound - US Dollar)**
**Key Factors:**
- **Bank of England** monetary policy
- **Brexit Impact**: Trade relationships post-Brexit
- **Economic Indicators**: UK inflation, employment
- **Political Stability**: Government policies

#### **USD-JPY (US Dollar - Japanese Yen)**
**Characteristics:**
- **Safe Haven**: JPY strengthens during global uncertainty
- **Bank of Japan**: Ultra-loose monetary policy
- **Carry Trade**: Popular due to low Japanese interest rates
- **Risk On/Off**: JPY weakens in risk-on environments

### **3. CURRENCY TRADING MECHANICS**

#### **Interest Rate Parity Theory**
**Formula**: F = S √ó (1 + rd) / (1 + rf)
- F = Forward rate
- S = Spot rate  
- rd = Domestic interest rate
- rf = Foreign interest rate

**Practical Application:**
If Indian rates > US rates ‚Üí INR should weaken in forward market

#### **Purchasing Power Parity (PPP)**
**Concept**: Same goods should cost same across countries (adjusted for exchange rate)
**Big Mac Index**: Popular PPP indicator using McDonald's prices globally
**Application**: Indicates if currency is over/undervalued

#### **Factors Affecting Exchange Rates**
1. **Interest Rate Differentials**: Higher rates attract capital
2. **Inflation Rates**: Lower inflation preserves value
3. **Economic Growth**: Stronger growth attracts investment
4. **Political Stability**: Reduces country risk
5. **Current Account Balance**: Trade surplus/deficit impact
6. **Speculation**: Short-term market sentiment

### **4. PRECIOUS METALS**

#### **Gold Trading**
**Gold Price Drivers:**
- **US Dollar Strength**: Inverse relationship typically
- **Real Interest Rates**: Negative real rates support gold
- **Inflation Expectations**: Gold as inflation hedge
- **Geopolitical Tensions**: Safe haven demand
- **Central Bank Policies**: QE policies support gold

**India-Specific Factors:**
- **Import Duties**: Government policy on gold imports
- **Festival Seasons**: Diwali, wedding seasons boost demand
- **Monsoon**: Good monsoon = higher rural gold demand
- **Currency**: Weaker INR makes gold expensive domestically

**Silver vs Gold:**
- **Industrial Demand**: Silver has more industrial applications
- **Volatility**: Silver more volatile than gold
- **Gold-Silver Ratio**: Historical range 60-80

### **9. HEDGING STRATEGIES**

#### **Currency Hedging**
**For Importers:**
- **Buy USD Futures**: Protect against INR weakening
- **Call Options**: Limited downside, unlimited upside

**For Exporters:**
- **Sell USD Futures**: Protect against INR strengthening
- **Put Options**: Limited downside, unlimited upside

#### **Commodity Hedging**
**For Jewelers (Gold):**
- **Buy Gold Futures**: Hedge inventory price risk
- **Long Call Options**: Participate in upside, limit downside

**For Airlines (Crude Oil):**
- **Buy Crude Futures**: Hedge fuel cost increase
- **Call Spreads**: Reduce premium cost of hedging

### **10. MACROECONOMIC LINKAGES**

#### **Currency Impact on Economy**
- **Weak Currency**: Helps exports, hurts imports
- **Strong Currency**: Helps imports, hurts exports
- **Tourism**: Strong currency attracts foreign tourists
- **FDI**: Stable currency encourages foreign investment

#### **Commodity Impact on Inflation**
- **Energy Prices**: Direct impact on transportation costs
- **Food Prices**: Agricultural commodity prices affect CPI
- **Metal Prices**: Impact infrastructure and manufacturing costs
- **Gold Prices**: Wealth effect on consumption

### **11. GLOBAL EVENTS & MARKET REACTIONS**

#### **Central Bank Policies**
- **Federal Reserve**: USD strength, global liquidity
- **European Central Bank**: EUR impact, global risk appetite
- **Bank of Japan**: JPY carry trades, global funding
- **RBI**: INR stability, domestic liquidity

#### **Geopolitical Events**
- **Trade Wars**: Currency volatility, commodity demand
- **Military Conflicts**: Safe haven demand (Gold, JPY, CHF)
- **Sanctions**: Commodity supply disruptions
- **Elections**: Policy uncertainty, market volatility
